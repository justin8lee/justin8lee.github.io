Music & Guitar

Guitar is my longest-running practice. I focus on tone, timing, and phrasing—building parts that fit the song and feel good to play. I like clean and edge-of-breakup sounds where dynamics matter. My practice aims to make progress visible: short routines, clear checkpoints, and recordings for feedback. When I teach, I prioritize clarity and confidence over speed, and help students connect technique to musical decisions.

The same approach applies to performance: keep time steady, play fewer but better notes, and listen to the whole band. I use simple tools—a metronome, a looper, and a phone mic—to test ideas quickly. On the theory side, I emphasize chord tones and voice leading before modes or large pattern sets. The goal is practical fluency across the neck and tasteful choices under real tempo and dynamics.

Focus Areas

  • Sound: pick attack, muting, and gain staging
  • Time: subdivisions, click games, and consistent pocket
  • Harmony: chord tones, guide tones, and voice-led lines
  • Practice: small daily sets with recordings for feedback

I share short clips on Instagram: @justin8music. For lessons or collaboration, feel free to reach out via email.
