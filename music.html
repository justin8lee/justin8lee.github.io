Music & Guitar

Guitar is where I practice consistency and taste. My approach centers on tone, touch, and timing: building a sound that serves the song, keeping hands relaxed, and locking into the pocket. I design short, focused routines—fifteen minutes that target a single constraint—because progress compounds when feedback is fast and goals are specific. In lessons, I translate concepts into plain language and small wins: one voicing at a time, one strum grid at a time, one clean bend at a time.

I’m most at home in clean and edge-of-breakup textures, using dynamics and right-hand articulation to shape phrases. On the theory side, I emphasize practical harmony and fretboard fluency over memorizing shapes in isolation. Students learn to hear movement first, then map it: chord tones, guide tones, and voice-led lines that actually sit well under the fingers. The same mindset informs my work outside music: define the problem, remove friction, and iterate with intention.

Practice Pillars

  • Sound: pick attack, muting, and consistent gain staging.
  • Time: subdivision grids, slow practice, and tasteful use of space.
  • Harmony: chord-tone targeting and voice leading over progressions.
  • Translation: turn riffs into reusable exercises and gig-ready parts.
